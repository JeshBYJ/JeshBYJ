### 姩菡

MS student at Shanghai Jiao Tong University. I work on speech large models and
multimodal LLMs — specifically on the places they quietly fail: hallucinated
sounds, speaker attribution, instruction data that looks clean but isn't.

My bias is empirical. Most things I build are small tools that make a
specific failure mode measurable — benchmarks, probes, curation pipelines —
because I learned pretty quickly that "looks like it works" and "works" are
very different things once you measure.

Outside of research I read a lot about audio perception, cook badly, and
maintain an ongoing suspicion of any metric that can't be bootstrapped.

---

#### Research interests

`Speech LLMs` &nbsp; `Multimodal LLMs` &nbsp; `Neural speech codecs` &nbsp;
`Faithfulness evaluation` &nbsp; `Data curation` &nbsp; `Probing / interpretability`

#### What I'm working on

- A benchmark for audio-visual LLM faithfulness with per-modality error attribution
- Probing what neural speech codec tokens actually encode (semantic vs acoustic)
- Streaming curation for multimodal instruction-tuning data
- Reading whatever new speech LLM just shipped on arXiv this week
